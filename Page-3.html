There are quite a number of solutions to this issue. Some of them include;

  1. Using better nutrient management techniques. This means that using things like fertilizers or manure at the correct amount, time and place
  2.  Planting field barriers or buffers like shrubs or trees can also be a very good solution to this issue considering the fact that they can prevent nutrient loss mostly if close to a water body. The barriers will most probably absorb the nutrients before it is gone into the water body
  3. Follow “The 4 Point Plan” provided by the Scottish government which is: reduce dirty water around the farm, improve nutrient use, carry out land risk assessment for slurry and manure, manage your water margins
  4. Keep a good distance between any water source from ditches to drinking water supplies by about 10 – 50 m. especially when using things like fertilizers, organic wastes, pesticides or any other chemicals
  5. Make sure that the sprayer operators are properly trained and have appropriate qualifications of competence and are properly maintained and often tested
  6. Do not use pesticides, veterinary medicines or any other chemicals if there is no identified need of it and keep it to a minimum
